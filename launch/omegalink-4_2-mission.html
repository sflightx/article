sFlightX is targeted to launch 10 OmegaLink v6 Experimental Satellites aboard Maya NET July 16, 2024 at 14:35 UTC. Version 6 Satellites carry working antennas to build a low latency communications on Low Earth Orbit.

Live webcast starts 10 minutes before the liftoff.

This mission, counts as sFlightX's 14th launch attempt and 13th orbital launch of 2024. Numerous upgrades are visible between today's flight and the previous one, the DSS-1 Mission.

The Pad being used is the Old Pad where Block 3 and Block 4 lifted off last April and May. During the static fire, 2 engines showed unfavorable conditions. These engines were produced as early prototype engine before flights at BSC has already commenced.

During the fire, 2 engines are showing unhealthy conditions, but teams decided to give this a liftoff.

A new landing sequence will debut this flight. After stage separation, the booster will not attempt a boostback burn. 6 minutes after the launch, the booster will attempt reentry burn, stripping its descent velocity over a half for a precise aerodynamic descent.

7 to 8 minutes after the flight, the booster will initiate the landing burn 5 kilometers above the ground, from 3,600 km/h.

With more and more flights, this give us confidence for a rapid, reusable rockets in Juno: New Origins in the future!
